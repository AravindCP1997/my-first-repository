Hedge Accounting

Changes in accounting required, when there is a hedge relationship between a hedging instrument and a hedged item, from their individual accounting, constitute the Hedge Accounting. When the changes so made, the financial statements reflect the following elements of hedge:

  1. Cost of Hedge
  2. Hedge Effectiveness
  3. Unrealised Gains or Losses

Need for Hedge Accounting

Cost of hedge

The excess of forward rate (INR 87.15/ USD) over spot rate (INR 83.00/ USD) is the forward premium (INR 4.15/ USD). When a forward contract is used as a hedging instrument, the forward premium is considered to be the cost of hedge. The cost of hedge is recognised in the Profit and Loss on a systematic basis.

Hedge Effectiveness

A hedge is effective when gain on hedging instrument equates loss on hedged item or vice versa, thereby, having zero impact in the Profit and Loss. When gain on hedging instrument does not equate loss on hedged item, the hedge is ineffective and impacts the Profit and Loss.

Unrealised Gains or Losses

On an interim date within a hedge period, there may be gains and losses reflecting a hedge ineffectiveness. The ineffectiveness (gain or loss) is temporary and unrealised, unless the entity settles the hedge at the interim date.

If the financial statements present such unrealised gains and losses separately, from the realised and provide information about their potential impact on the Profit and Loss, it is more relevant for the decision-making needs of the users. For that purpose, the unrealised gains or losses are recognised and accumulated in Other Comprehensive Income and recycled to the Profit and Loss, when realised.

Forecast

The forward contract forecasts the USD to appreciate 5% to INR 87.15/USD by 30 June. If so, by 31 March, USD will appreciate 2.5% to INR 85.075/ USD (spot rate) and forward rate will remain at INR 87.15/USD. The foreign exchange loss on trade payables will be INR 2.075/USD and gain on forward contracts INR 0.00/USD. Resulting in a net loss on the hedge of INR 2.075/USD, which reflects the cost of hedge for the period (INR 4.15/USD * 3/6).

Actual

However, on 31 March, spot rate becomes INR 86.00/USD and forward rate for 30 June becomes INR 88.15/ USD. The foreign exchange loss on trade payables is INR 3.00/ USD and gain on forward contract INR 1.00/USD. Resulting in a net loss on the hedge, up to 31 March of INR 2.00/USD. This is INR 0.075/USD less than the cost of the hedge (forecasted) up to 31 March (INR 2.075/USD).

However, the gain of INR 0.075/USD is unrealised, unless the entity settles the payables and the forward contract on 31 March itself, instead of holding up to 30 June. This potential gain of INR 0.075/ USD is presented separately from the realised gains/ losses.
