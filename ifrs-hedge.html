Need for Hedge Accounting

Cost of hedge

The excess of forward rate (INR 87 15 / over spot rate (INR 83 00 / is the forward premium (INR 4 15 / When a forward contract is used as a hedging instrument, the forward premium is considered as the cost of hedge The cost of hedge is recognised in the Profit or Loss on a systematic basis.

Hedge Effectiveness

A hedge is effective when gain on hedging instrument equates loss on hedged item or vice versa, thereby, having zero impact in the Profit or Loss When gain on hedging instrument does not equate loss on hedged item, the hedge is ineffective and impacts the Profit or Loss.

Unrealised Gains or Losses

On an interim date within a hedge period, there may be gains and losses reflecting a hedge ineffectiveness The ineffectiveness ( losses) is unrealised, unless the entity settles the hedge at the interim date.

If the financial statements present such unrealised gains losses separately, from the realised gains losses and provide information about their potential impact on the Profit or Loss, it is more relevant for the decision making needs of the users For that purpose, the unrealised gains or losses are recognised and accumulated in Other Comprehensive Income and recycled to the Profit or Loss, when realised.

Need for Hedge Accounting

The forward contract forecasts the USD to appreciate 5% to INR 87.15/USD by 30 June 2025. If so, by 31 Mar 2025, USD will appreciate 2.5% to INR 85.075/ USD (spot rate) and forward rate will remain at INR 87.15/USD. The foreign exchange loss on trade payables will be INR 2.075/USD and gain on forward contracts INR 0.00/USD. Resulting in a net loss on the hedge of INR 2.075/USD, which reflects the cost of hedge for the period (INR 4.15/USD * 3/6).

However, on 31 Mar 2025, spot rate becomes INR 86.00/USD and forward rate for 30 June 2025 becomes INR 88.15/ USD. The foreign exchange loss on trade payables is INR 3.00/ USD and gain on forward contract INR 1.00/USD.

Resulting in a net loss on the hedge, up to 31 Mar 2025 of INR 2.00/USD. This is INR 0.075/USD less than the cost of the hedge up to 31 Mar 2025 (INR 2.075/USD).

However, the gain of INR 0.075/USD is unrealised, unless the entity settles the payables and the forward contract on 31 Mar 2025 itself, instead of holding up to 30 June 2025. This potential gain of INR 0.075/ USD is presented separately from the realised gains/ losses.
