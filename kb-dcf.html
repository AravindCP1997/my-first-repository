Discounted Cash Flow (DCF)

Discounted cash flow is the financial valuation method used to estimate the value of an investment or project based on its expected future cash flows.

Financial worth of the investment or project is determined by discounting cash flows arising from it.

DCF techniques commonly used include:

Key Components of DCF

  • Future Cash Flows
  • Discount Rate
  • Present Value Calculation

Steps in DCF Analysis

  1. Forecast future cash flows
  2. Select an appropriate discount rate
  3. Calculate the present value of future cash flows
  4. Sum the present values to get the total value

Applications of DCF

DCF is widely used in finance for valuing companies, projects, and investments, helping investors make informed decisions.
