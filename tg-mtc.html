International Taxation

Model Tax Conventions

Tax Convention

A Tax Convention, also called Tax Treaty or Double Taxation Avoidance Agreement, is a bilateral agreement between two countries, aimed at the elimination of double taxation with respect to taxes on income and on capital, and without creating opportunities for tax avoidance or tax evasion through profit shifting.

A Tax Convention mutually agrees between two countries, the taxing rights on income and on capital of a taxpayer, where the source of income or location of capital is one country (can be referred to as the source state) and the taxpayer is resident in another country (can be referred to as the resident state).

Double Taxation and Profit Shifting

Double Taxation: Levy of tax by two or more jurisdictions or states on the same income or capital of a taxpayer. This usually happens when both the resident state and source state levy tax. Double taxation is a situation of increased tax burden to a taxpayer.

Profit Shifting: Tax avoidance strategy where multinational enterprises artificially shift profit from high-tax jurisdiction or state to low-tax or no-tax jurisdiction or state, minimising its global tax liability.

Model Tax Convention

A Model Tax Convention is a template Tax Convention used by countries to develop Bilateral Tax Conventions between them. Model Tax Convention is only a guide for development of Tax Conventions and is not binding on countries, by itself.

There are two primary models of Tax Conventions:

  • United Nations (UN) Model Double Taxation Convention
  • Organisation for Economic Co-operation and Development (OECD) Model Tax Convention

Taxation of Capital

UN Model

  • Immovable property: Taxed in the State in which the property is situated.
  • Permanent establishment or fixed base: Movable property forming part of the business property of a permanent establishment which an enterprise of a State has in another State or movable property pertaining to a fixed base available to a resident of a State in another State for the purpose of performing independent personal services may be taxed in that other State.
  • Enterprises operating ships or aircrafts: Capital of an enterprise of a State that operates ships or aircraft in international traffic, represented by such ships or aircraft, and by movable property pertaining to the operation of such ships or aircraft, shall be taxable only in that State.
  • Others: All other elements of capital of a resident of a State shall be taxable only in that State.

OECD Model

  • Immovable property: Taxed in the State in which the property is situated.
  • Permanent establishment: Movable property forming part of the business property of a permanent establishment which an enterprise of a State has in another State may be taxed in that other State.
  • Enterprises operating ships or aircrafts: Capital of an enterprise of a State that operates ships or aircraft in international traffic represented by such ships or aircraft, and by movable property pertaining to the operation of such ships or aircraft, shall be taxable only in that State.
  • Others: All other elements of capital of a resident of a State shall be taxable only in that State.

Article 22 of respective Conventions

Taxation of Associated Enterprises

Associated Enterprises

  • When one enterprise of a State participates directly or indirectly in the management, control or capital of an enterprise of another State, or
  • When the same persons participate directly or indirectly in the management, control or capital of an enterprise of a State and an enterprise of another State

Taxation

When conditions between the two associated enterprises, in their commercial or financial relations, differ from those which would be made between independent enterprises, then any profits, which would have accrued to one of the enterprises, but by reason of those conditions, have not so accrued, may be included in the profits of that enterprise and taxed accordingly.

Article 9 of respective Conventions

Methods for Elimination of Double Tax

Exemption Method

  • Where a resident of a State derives income or owns capital which may be taxed in another State, in accordance with the provisions of the Convention, then the first-mentioned State shall exempt such income or capital from tax.
  • Where a resident of a State derives items of income which may be taxed in another State, in accordance with the provisions of Articles 10, 11, 12, and 12A in UN Model convention or Articles 10 and 11 in OECD Model Convention, then the first-mentioned State shall allow as a deduction from the tax on the income of that resident an amount equal to the tax paid in that other State.

(Deduction of tax shall not exceed the tax before such deduction, attributable to the income or capital derived from the other state.)

Credit Method

  • Where a resident of a State derives income or owns capital which may be taxed in another State in accordance with the provisions of the Convention, the first-mentioned State shall allow as deduction from tax of that resident an amount equal to tax paid in the other State.

(Deduction of tax shall not exceed the tax before such deduction, attributable to the income or capital derived from the other state.)
